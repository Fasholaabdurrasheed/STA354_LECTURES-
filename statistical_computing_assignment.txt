Question 1
The three question an analyst would ask when working with one-sample data

1. What is the central tendency of the dataset?
This involves calculating the mean or median of the data.

2. Does the sample mean differ from a hypothesized population mean?
This is usually tested using a one-sample t-test.

3. Does the data follow a normal distribution?
This determines whether parametric or nonparametric tests should be used.

QUESTION 5

Difference between parametric and nonparametric tests:

Parametric Tests:
Assume normal distribution and involve parameters such as mean and variance.

Example in R:
t.test(Y, mu=50)

Nonparametric Tests:
Do not assume normal distribution and are based on ranks.

Example in R:
wilcox.test(X, mu=1)
